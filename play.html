I layed down by the river

Palm Sunday, 14 April 2019
Mary Magdalene / Cecilia Vidal
Angel / Erin Brady

Written by Cecilia Vidal
Sound by K. Mulhern
Photos by Parker Camp, Ryan Bongo
Costumes, set, and production by Sunday Collect

In the New Testament, Mary Magdalene is depicted as a composite character of many female archetypes, a burden for any woman to carry. "I layed down by the river" depicts a young woman on the cusp of womanhood, confronting her vulnerability and facing her independent and unknown future. The play is inspired by the songs of early 20th century Appalachian folk band the Carter Family, and contains some dialogue taken from The Golden Legend. Compiled by Blessed Jacobus de Varagine, The Golden Legend is a 13th century collection of speculative stories (fan fiction) of the life of the saints, including that of Mary Magdalene and her life after Jesus.
